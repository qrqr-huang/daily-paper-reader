## 摘要
由于内部耗散，电池放电受输出功率与能量转换效率间的基本权衡支配。本文证明，这种权衡普遍产生抛物线包络$P\proptoη(1-η)$。最大功率时的效率恰为二分之一，反映了有限时间热力学中著名的半卡诺极限。为将此界限扩展至实际操作规则，我们制定了同时满足实时负载需求和全局放电截止时间的多阶段恒流放电调度。通过Karush-Kuhn-Tucker条件的解析求解，揭示了一个极其简洁的最优策略：$I_{i}^{\star}=\max(I_{i}^{-},I_{0})$。在此规则下，受外部需求限制的阶段恰好以其最小所需电流运行，而所有其他阶段均提升至由截止时间约束确定的统一基线$I_{0}$。通过追踪耗散-时间帕累托前沿，我们量化了内阻如何改变操作边界并锐化权衡拐角。该分析为电池管理系统的调度层建立了严格的热力学基线，为纳入温度和荷电状态依赖性的非线性模型提供了自然延伸。

## Abstract
Battery discharging is governed by a fundamental trade-off between output power and energy conversion efficiency due to internal dissipation. In this paper, we demonstrate that such a trade-off universally yields a parabolic envelope $P\proptoη(1-η)$. The efficiency at maximum power is exactly one half, mirroring the well-known half-Carnot limit in finite-time thermodynamics. To extend this bound into practical operational rules, we formulate a multistage constant-current discharging (MSCD) schedule subject to simultaneous real-time load demands and a global discharging deadline. Analytical resolution via the Karush--Kuhn--Tucker conditions reveals a remarkably compact optimal policy: $I_{i}^{\star}=\max(I_{i}^{-},I_{0})$. Under this rule, stages limited by external demand run exactly at their minimum required currents, while all remaining stages are elevated to a uniform baseline $I_{0}$ fixed by the deadline constraint. By tracing the dissipation--time Pareto front, we quantify how internal resistance shifts the operational boundaries and sharpens the trade-off corner. This analysis establishes a rigorous thermodynamic baseline for the scheduling layer of battery management systems, offering natural extensions to nonlinear models incorporating temperature and state-of-charge dependencies.

## 摘要
本文针对在轮询协议通信约束下运行的离散时间线性随机奇异系统，提出了一种风险敏感卡尔曼滤波框架。由于网络带宽有限，每个采样时刻只能传输部分测量值，导致测量结构呈现周期变化。利用Weierstrass标准型，将奇异系统转化为等效的增广状态空间模型，得到一个轮询诱导的周期系统。通过贝叶斯公式和最小化指数二次代价函数，为该周期系统开发了递归风险敏感卡尔曼滤波器，从而获得了原奇异系统的递归滤波方程。为了增强对建模不确定性和干扰的鲁棒性，引入了一种自适应风险敏感机制，其中风险参数根据可用的协方差信息在线调整。这种自适应策略在调整对当前估计不确定性的风险敏感程度的同时，保证了预测协方差矩阵的正定性。此外，利用周期系统的可观测性和可控制性概念，建立了确保滤波器稳定性的充分条件。当风险敏感参数消失时，所提出的框架退化为奇异系统的标准卡尔曼滤波器；当奇异矩阵简化为单位矩阵时，恢复为标准风险敏感卡尔曼滤波器。最后，通过数值结果展示了所提方法与标准卡尔曼滤波器相比的有效性、鲁棒性和改进的估计性能。

## Abstract
This paper develops a risk sensitive (RS) Kalman filtering framework for discrete-time linear stochastic singular systems operating under communication constraints imposed by a round-robin protocol. Due to limited network bandwidth, only a subset of the available measurements can be transmitted at each sampling instant, resulting in a periodically varying measurement structure. By employing the Weierstrass canonical form (WCF), the singular system is transformed into an equivalent augmented state space model, yielding a round-robin induced periodic system (RRIPS). A recursive risk sensitive Kalman filter (RSKF) is then developed for the RRIPS through a Bayesian formulation and the minimization of an exponential quadratic cost function, from which the recursive filtering equations are obtained for the original singular system. To enhance robustness against modeling uncertainties and disturbances, an adaptive RS mechanism is introduced in which the risk parameter is adjusted online according to the available covariance information. This adaptive strategy guarantees the positive definiteness of the predicted covariance matrix while adjusting the degree of risk sensitivity to the prevailing estimation uncertainty. Furthermore, sufficient conditions ensuring the filter stability are established using the observability and controllability concepts of periodic systems. The proposed framework reduces to the standard KF for singular systems when the RS parameter vanishes and recovers the standard RSKF when the singular matrix reduces to the identity matrix. Finally, numerical results are presented to demonstrate the effectiveness, robustness, and improved estimation performance of the proposed approach in comparison with the standard KF.
